care:
- "Spray trees with dormant oil to control San Jose scale, aphid eggs, mite eggs."
- "Prune 15–20% last year’s growth to let light in."
- "Remove diseased/broken limbs."
problem-opportunities:
- "<b>Priority problems</b> include: production problems due to poor rootstock, pollination, planting (bed preparation), training and pruning, variety selection, and IPM for Apples: Codling moth, powdery mildew, apple scab, fire blight, wholly apple aphid, San Jose scale, green fruit worm, European red mite, green apple aphids, Bermuda grass."
- "<b>Priority opportunities</b> include training nurserymen in improved rootstock management, better match varieties with growing regions (See PHDP), and improved water management."
- "These draft priority problems are based on feedback from agricultural consultants, Afghans specialists, and Tom Brown."
